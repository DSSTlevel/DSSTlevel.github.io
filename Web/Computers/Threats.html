Main

Botnets – Network of privatised computers infected with malicious software and controlled as a group with owners’ acknowledgement
DoS – An attack that is meant to force shutdown a machine or networking. This is caused by flooding the network with traffic or sending information that triggers a crash
DDoS – Multiple systems target a single network with a DoS attack

Hacking

Cross-site scripting – Hacker stores malicious in the data sent from websites search or contact form.
Password-cracking software – Process of recovering passwords from data that has been stored or transmitted by a computer system.
SQL-injection – Common attack that uses malicious SQL code to backend database manipulation to access information.

Malware

Viruses – Computer code designed to spread from device to device. Self-copying threats are usually designed to damage a device or steal data.
Trojans – Disguises itself as legitimate code or software. The attacker is then liable to carry out any action that a legitimate user could perform, e.g., delete files.
Worms – Subset of trojan horse malware that can self-replicate from one computer to another without human activation after breaching a system.
Remote Access Trojans – Designed to allow an attacker to remotely control an infected computer.
Key Loggers – Track activities of a keyboard (e.g., track password inputs).
Ransomware – Prevents you from accessing your device and the data stored on it, usually encrypting the files.
Spyware – Software that installs itself on the computer and starts monitoring your online behaviour with your acknowledgement.
Adware – Unwanted software designed to throw adverts up on your screen. Most often within a web browser.

Malicious spam

Phishing – Attacker attempts to trick users into doing “the wrong thing”
Spear Phishing – Email or electronic scam targeted towards a specific individual, organisation or business.
Smishing – Phishing attack carried out over mobile text messaging.
Vishing – Phishing attack carried out over the phone.
