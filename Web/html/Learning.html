Sources of Knowledge

Main Sources

Forums – Online engagement between a community where solutions are shared from one main problem
Textbooks – Standard work for the study of a particular subject
Academic papers – Journals and Contains original research results
White papers – Informative and persuasive report on a specific subject that gives a problem and solution
Supplier literature – Documentation provided a supplier to a customer, typically relates to products or services
Search engines – A program that searches and identifies items in a database, corresponding to keywords and characters
Websites – Related web pages located under a single domain
Blogs – Regularly updated website or web page that is written in conversational style
Wikis – Database/Website that is collaborated on by a community of users
Social media – Enable users to share content
Conferences – Formal meeting of people with a shared interest
Developer kits – Assistance for production of a specific items
E-learning – Learning or training through online resources
Subject matter expert – Provides the knowledge and expertise in a specific subject for a project/program

Validation

Author expertise – Experience that the author has in that specific field Bias – Prejudice for or against one person or group, especially if considered unfair Evidence – Information or facts used to show whether something is true or valid Subjectivity – Quality of being based on or influenced by personal feelings, tastes or opinions Context – Circumstances that form an environment of an event, statement or idea, so it can be fully understood Intended audience – Dedicated interest that people follow maybe interested in the product or service. Date of publication – The date that it was publicly visible Corroboration of sources – Compare information provided by 2 different sources. Citations – Quote from or a reference to the original author
